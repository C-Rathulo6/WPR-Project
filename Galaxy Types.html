    • Galaxies are vast collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, each with unique characteristics. The main types of galaxies are:

    Spiral Galaxies: These have a flat, rotating disk with spiral arms and a central bulge. The Milky Way is a spiral galaxy.

    Elliptical Galaxies: Shaped like elongated spheres, these galaxies have little gas and dust and mostly contain older stars.

    Irregular Galaxies: These lack a distinct shape and often result from galaxy collisions or gravitational interactions.

    Lenticular Galaxies: These are a blend of spiral and elliptical galaxies, featuring a central bulge and a disk but no spiral arms.

    Each type of galaxy plays a crucial role in the universe's structure and evolution. Understanding these types helps astronomers learn more about the cosmos and our place within it.
